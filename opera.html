Parker Kennedy Light Opera

H.M.S Pinnafore

Act II, SCENE. Night. Awning removed. Moonlight. CAPTAIN discovered singing on poop deck, and accompanying himself on a mandolin. LITTLE BUTTERCUP seated on quarterdeck, gazing sentimentally at him.

CAPT.

Incomprehensible as her utterances are, I nevertheless feel that they are dictated by a sincere regard for me. But to what new misery is she referring? Time alone can tell!

SIR JOSEPH.

Captain Corcoran, I am much disappointed with your daughter. In fact, I don't think she will do.

CAPT.

At last my fond hopes are to be crowned. My only daughter is to be the bride of a Cabinet Minister. The prospect is Elysian.

(During this speech DICK DEADEYE has entered.)

BOTH.

The merry cat-o'-nine-tails and the tar!

CAPT.

Dick Deadeye—I thank you for your warning—I will at once take means to arrest their flight. This boat cloak will afford me ample disguise—So!

(Envelops himself in a mysterious cloak, holding it before his face.)

SIR JOSEPH.

Don't be ashamed of that. Your position as a topman is a very exalted one.

Enter JOSEPHINE;

she rushes to RALPH'S arm

(SIR JOSEPH horrified.)
